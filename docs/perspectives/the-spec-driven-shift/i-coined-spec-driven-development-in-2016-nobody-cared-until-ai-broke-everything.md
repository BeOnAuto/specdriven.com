# I Coined 'Spec-Driven Development' in 2016. Nobody Cared Until AI Broke Everything.

![I Coined 'Spec-Driven Development' in 2016. Nobody Cared Until AI Broke Everything.](/images/perspectives/i-coined-spec-driven-development-in-2016-nobody-cared-until-ai-broke-everything.png)

I started using the term "spec-driven development" in 2016. Bought specdriven.com in 2020. Could see where things were heading.

For years: nothing. Crickets.

I posted "where are your specs?" on LinkedIn once. A very experienced engineer, someone I respect, basically said: "If you asked that to my team, they'd just stare at you. They engineer. They have CI/CD. They have good practices. What even is this 'specs' thing?"

Honestly? I got it. The nuance wasn't widely understood. People saw tests and code. Two things. Nobody thought of specifications as a separate, independent concern. And in fairness, the line is blurry. Code IS a kind of specification. But it's lossy. Code tells you how something works. It doesn't tell you what it's supposed to do or why anyone decided it should work that way.

Acceptance criteria on a ticket? That's closer. But it dies when the ticket closes. Six months later it's buried in some archived sprint and nobody will ever read it again.

The people who got it right away were BDD practitioners and TDD veterans. They already lived in a world where specs, tests, and the system under test were three different things. They'd hear "spec-driven" and just nod.

Everyone else thought I was adding process for the sake of process.

Then AI happened.

Suddenly every team on earth is dealing with the exact problem I'd been going on about: if you don't capture your design intent somewhere durable, you lose control of your system. The safety net of human judgment, the developer who pauses and asks "are you sure?", is gone. AI just builds.

The conversation I couldn't get anyone to have in 2016 is now the most important conversation in software development.

I'd been developing these ideas long before the term though. Early in my coaching career I came up with the distinction between domain specs and component specs. Domain specs are about your business behaviors and interactions. Component specs are about technical modules. Two different things with different audiences.

That framework got the same reception. Too abstract. Just write the code.

Timing matters with ideas. But so does showing up early and staying.

Sometimes the right idea just needs the right moment. The spec-driven conversation that couldn't get traction in 2016 is now the most important conversation in software development.
