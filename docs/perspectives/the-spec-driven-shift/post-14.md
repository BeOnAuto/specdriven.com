# Give Me the Freedom of a Tight Brief. Why Specs Are the Ultimate AI Prompt.

I was auditing a media agency years ago and a creative director said something I've never forgotten.

"Give me the freedom of a tight brief."

This was an artist. Not someone who wanted a blank canvas and infinite possibilities. They wanted clarity. Constraints. A specific problem to solve within well-defined boundaries.

Because a wide-open brief is actually paralyzing. When anything is possible, you spend your energy figuring out what to do. When the boundaries are clear, you spend it on doing it well.

AI is the same.

Without a tight brief, an AI coding tool exercises its creativity everywhere. It picks architectural patterns. Chooses data structures. Decides naming conventions. Makes trade-offs between performance and readability. Selects error handling strategies. All silently. All without considering what you've already decided or what conventions your team follows.

You get something that works. But it's not what you designed. Because you didn't design it. You described a vague wish and the AI made every design decision on your behalf.

With a tight brief, with actual specifications, the AI gets to do what it's genuinely good at. Implementation. Fast, accurate, tireless implementation within bounds you've set. That's where AI shines. It's a great implementer. It just needs to know what to implement.

I call this the holy trinity of spec-driven development. Specifications: the communication of your design. Tests: the executable proof that the specs are being met. System under test: the implementation, which could be AI-generated code or anything else. It doesn't matter how it works under the hood. Could be pixie dust. As long as it meets the specs.

When you look at it that way, prompt engineering is really just specification by another name. A good prompt is just a spec. But most people's prompts are the equivalent of telling that creative director "make something cool." No constraints. No brief. No clarity.

Specs are the ultimate prompt. They give it the clarity to do good work.
