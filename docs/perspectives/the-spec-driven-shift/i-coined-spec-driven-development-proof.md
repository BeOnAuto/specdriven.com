# I Coined 'Spec-Driven Development': Proof and Prior Art

This is the supporting evidence behind ["I Coined 'Spec-Driven Development' in 2016. Nobody Cared Until AI Broke Everything"](./i-coined-spec-driven-development-in-2016-nobody-cared-until-ai-broke-everything). The original post is the personal narrative. This page is the receipts.

This is a working page. It will grow as more proof points surface, as more pushback comes in, and as collaborators from the 2010-2024 period contribute first-hand records.

## What "I coined it" actually means.

I have never claimed to have invented spec-driven development. The conceptual lineage runs back through Bertrand Meyer's Design by Contract (1986) and Tony Hoare's logic (1969). The phrase *"specification-driven development"* itself appears in academic literature from 2004 onward in the formal methods community, predating my industrial use by twelve years. The full term genealogy is on the [SDD Origins](/origins) page. The full practice lineage is on the [Timeline](/timeline).

What I did do, in 2016, was start using the phrase "spec-driven development" in my industrial consulting work, and continue using it commercially through the rest of the decade and into the 2020s. As far as the public record reflects, that was the earliest sustained industrial use of the phrase. The position is first sustained industrial use, not invention.

## The "not the inventor" disclaimer, on record.

When someone called me "the inventor of SDD" in [a recent LinkedIn thread](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7452751669580156928?commentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28activity%3A7452751669580156928%2C7452932772358201345%29&replyUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28activity%3A7452751669580156928%2C7453004412429578241%29), I corrected them on the spot:

> *"Thanks for the shout out. I wouldn't call myself the inventor of SDD though. I first stated it in 2016 but it was an echo of what the industry has been cooking since the 60's!"*

That's the position. It hasn't moved. If anyone surfaces evidence of an earlier sustained industrial use of the phrase, it will be added here and the framing on the rest of the site will be updated to match.

## The 2016-2024 industrial record (Wayback evidence).

These are the public artifacts I can point to.

- **2016: [simian.io](https://web.archive.org/web/20160406163643/http://simian.io/).** First specs-focused product, built on the conviction that BDD scenarios deserved a tool of their own.
- **2018: [xspecs.io](https://web.archive.org/web/20180829191612/https://www.xspecs.io/).** Successor product. The site states verbatim: *"these specifications are used as the single source of truth to drive development."* This is a dated, public, commercial use of the spec-driven framing.
- **2018: [Embracing Specifications](https://gist.github.com/SamHatoum/388c6ff663eb86ef3463a251b48f2db5).** Public gist collecting some of the early thinking on the domain-spec / component-spec distinction.
- **2020: specdriven.com purchased.** The domain has been in continuous use as a spec-driven resource since.
- **2021: [Spec-Driven Development](https://gist.github.com/SamHatoum/e3789342ec5329ed5b9a8fecd64346f3).** Public gist using the phrase directly.
- **2023: [xspecs.io homepage (archived)](https://web.archive.org/web/20230331055318/https://www.xspecs.io/).** The site openly frames the work as the *"spec-driven revolution."*

The bulk of the actual practitioner work happened inside private consulting engagements between roughly 2010 and 2024 at Xolvio. There are no public records of those engagements. First-hand accounts from collaborating engineers are being collected and will be added here as they come in.

## The academic prior art (where the term first appeared in print).

The phrase "specification-driven development" appears in print earlier than the 2016 industrial use, in a different community.

- Ostroff, J., Makalsky, D., Paige, R. (2004). *Agile Specification-Driven Development*. XP 2004, Springer LNCS.
- Paige, R., Ostroff, J. (2004). *Specification-Driven Design with Eiffel and Agents for Teaching Lightweight Formal Methods*. TFM 2004, Springer LNCS 3294.

Both papers come out of the formal methods and Eiffel teaching communities. They argue that tests and contracts are both specifications, that they should be intertwined with analysis, design and implementation, and that all of this can be done agilely. Different community, different scope, but the phrase exists.

I came to this material through [a comment from Jason Gorman](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7446953774734061569?commentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28activity%3A7446953774734061569%2C7453774863204401152%29) under one of my 2026 posts. His one-liner: *"Everything in software engineering's older than you think."* Gold.

The Ostroff papers are now properly cited on [SDD Origins](/origins) and on the [Timeline](/timeline).
