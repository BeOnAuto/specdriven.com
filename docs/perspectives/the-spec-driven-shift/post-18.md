# When Coding Becomes Instant, Specs Are All That Matters

"This is just waterfall with extra steps."

I get this one a lot. Let me deal with it, and then I'll give you a thought experiment that I think settles it.

Waterfall's problem wasn't specs. It was feedback speed. You'd spec everything, build everything, and discover everything was wrong. Months later. The cost of being wrong was catastrophic because you'd invested months before getting any signal.

Spec-driven development with AI is the opposite. The feedback cycle is minutes. Write a spec. AI implements it. Tests verify. You see the result. Adjust. AI re-implements. Minutes, not months.

TDD was also spec-first. BDD was spec-first. Writing acceptance criteria was spec-first. Nobody called those waterfall. They were small-batch practices. The only thing limiting batch size was how fast humans could implement, which forced everyone to work in the tiniest possible units.

AI is removing that constraint.

Here's the thought experiment. What if coding was truly instant? Describe what you want, implementation materializes in zero time. What would remain?

Only the spec. 100% of your effort on design. Zero on implementation.

Would you call that waterfall? Or would you call it pure design work?

AI is pushing us toward that world. Not there yet. But the trajectory is clear. Implementation cost dropping toward zero. What remains is the thinking. And the record of that thinking is the specification.

And here's what makes it not waterfall: the feedback is instant too. In waterfall, you couldn't validate your spec for months. Now you validate it in seconds. Write it, see it implemented, realize it's wrong, fix it. The discovery cycle is just as compressed as the implementation cycle.

So the real question isn't "should I write specs?" It's: can you afford not to? Because the alternative is trusting an amnesiac AI to hold every design decision you've ever made. All your intent. All your constraints. Maintained by something with no memory between sessions.

When coding becomes instant, the only thing left to differentiate your software is the quality of your specifications. That moment is closer than most people think.
