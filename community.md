# Community

Spec-Driven Development is bigger than any one tool, team, or dialect. It's a growing movement of practitioners who believe that specifications deserve to be first-class citizens in software development.

## Join the Conversation

The best way to connect with other spec-driven practitioners is on Discord. Share your experience, ask questions, discuss ideas, and help shape the future of how software gets specified.

[Join Discord →](https://discord.gg/onauto)

## Submit a Spec Dialect

Are you building a structured specification language? We want to hear about it.

A spec dialect is more than a workshop technique or a meeting format. To qualify, it should have:

- **A structured format** with a defined schema or grammar
- **Machine-parseable syntax** that tooling can process
- **Behavioral specification** — it describes what a system should do, not just facilitates discussion
- **A path to execution** — a relationship to tests, code generation, or validation

If your work meets these criteria, reach out on Discord and let's talk about featuring it on this site.
